Stakeholder Analysis · Framework 01

Structural Operational Layers

Analysts classify stakeholders across three strategic layers to track where requirements originate and how authority is distributed.

LAYER 01 / 03
Internal Stakeholders

Individuals working directly inside the corporate entity who manage operations or build the solution path.

  • Employees: Frontline users executing day-to-day work tasks
  • Managers: Business unit leaders controlling resource allocation
  • IT Teams: Engineers, developers, and testers building the system
LAYER 02 / 03
External Stakeholders

Entities outside the business boundary who interact with the system or consume its outputs.

  • Customers: Primary commercial users exchanging value for services
  • Suppliers: Upstream partners supplying materials or infrastructure
  • Integrators: API providers or third-party technical systems
LAYER 03 / 03
Regulators & Governance

Organizations that enforce statutory compliance or strict operational rules over system execution.

  • Regulators: Statutory bodies enforcing legal compliance
  • Auditors: Independent compliance verification authorities
  • Standards Bodies: Technical or professional standards setters
📍 South Africa · Local Application Note

Systems processing personal client data must adhere to mandates enforced by the Information Regulator, ensuring full architectural compliance with the Protection of Personal Information Act (POPIA). This falls under the Regulators & Governance layer.

Accountability Framework · Framework 03

RASCI Matrix Blueprint

The RASCI model maps clear ownership and task boundaries across a project to prevent accountability gaps.

R · Responsible
Operational Executor
The explicit design resource or delivery technician who builds the artifact or directly performs the task. There can be multiple R's.
A · Accountable
Final Approving Owner
The single individual with sign-off authority and ultimate ownership of the quality outcome. Rule: Only one "A" per task — no exceptions.
S · Support
Hands-On Helper
Team members who supply active secondary labour, assets, or data streams to aid the Responsible party in completing their work.
C · Consulted
Subject Matter Expert
Experts or compliance roles providing targeted feedback before work is finalised. This is two-way communication — their input shapes the output.
I · Informed
Unidirectional Monitor
Stakeholders notified of progress or final completions. They receive information but do not actively participate or provide feedback in the workflow.
⚠ Critical Rule

The "A" (Accountable) role must have exactly one person assigned per task. Shared accountability creates no accountability. The A-role holder also inherits responsibility for ensuring all R, S, C, and I parties are correctly engaged.

Worldview Analysis · Framework 04

CATWOE Analytical Framework

When departments clash over design priorities, BAs use CATWOE to uncover and reconcile conflicting underlying worldviews.

C
Letter C
Customers
The immediate end-users, beneficiaries, or impacted entities within the transformation loop. Those who experience the outcome — positively or negatively.
A
Letter A
Actors
The operational staff members who execute tasks and maintain daily systemic workflows. Those doing the work inside the transformation.
T
Letter T
Transformation
The core business loop converting raw input states into optimised corporate outputs. The "what changes" — the value-creating process itself.
W
Letter W
Weltanschauung
The distinct worldview or underlying belief system that makes the transformation valuable to a specific stakeholder. The "why it matters" — often the root of conflict.
O
Letter O
Owner
The individual or steering body holding the financial budget and executive authority to approve, modify, or halt the system entirely.
E
Letter E
Environmental Constraints
Fixed external limits the system must comply with — such as POPIA laws, structural budgets, infrastructure ceilings, or regulatory boundaries.
